## UP Poultry Industry: Size और Importance

उत्तर प्रदेश भारत के poultry production में एक significant player है।

**Key facts (DADF 2024-25):**
- UP में commercial broiler production: ~1.4 billion kg annually (estimate)
- UP का national share: approximately 7–8% of India's total broiler output
- Major producing districts: Gorakhpur, Bareilly, Lucknow-Unnao belt, Agra-Mathura belt
- **Gorakhpur belt** (Gorakhpur, Deoria, Kushinagar, Basti, Sant Kabir Nagar, Maharajganj): UP की poultry production का ~12–15%

UP का broiler market कई कारणों से unique है:
1. **Large local demand:** 25 million+ population in Gorakhpur division alone
2. **Proximity to Bihar:** Bihar को significant supply जाती है
3. **Andhra Pradesh influence:** AP और Telangana से UP में significant interstate supply
4. **Festival concentration:** Eid, Holi, Dussehra — major demand peaks
5. **Seasonal weather extremes:** May–July में 45°C+, December–January में 5°C — both extremes affect production

## 2025: पिछले साल क्या हुआ? (Recap)

2025 का साल UP poultry किसानों के लिए volatile था।

### Q1 2025 (January–March): Strong Start

- Average Gorakhpur APMC price: ₹168–185/kg
- Demand driver: Winter peak demand + Holi festival (March)
- Supply: Normal — no major disruptions
- **Result:** Good margins for farmers who sold correctly

### Q2 2025 (April–June): Heat Stress + HPAI Scare

- April: Sudden heat wave — production disrupted in Deoria, Kushinagar
- May: HPAI H5N1 detected in Maharajganj district → 48-hour price crash (₹182 → ₹151 in 3 days)
- June: Market recovery partial — ₹158–165 range
- **Result:** Farmers who had early alert (FlockIQ) avoided crash. Others lost ₹15–₹25/kg

### Q3 2025 (July–September): Monsoon Recovery

- July: Post-HPAI clearance, market gradually recovering
- August–September: Demand steady — Onam, Ganesh Chaturthi consumption
- Price range: ₹155–₹172/kg
- **Result:** Moderate. Transport challenges in flood-affected areas (Gorakhpur floods August)

### Q4 2025 (October–December): Best Quarter

- October: Dussehra/Navratri period — cautious demand (partial vegetarian festival)
- November–December: Winter peak demand. Eid ul-Adha period.
- Price range: ₹175–₹196/kg (December peak)
- **Result:** Best quarter. Farmers with birds ready in November–December did very well.

**2025 full-year average Gorakhpur price: ₹168/kg (estimated)**

## H2 2026 Outlook: क्या उम्मीद करें?

### July–August 2026: Monsoon Impact

**Positive factors:**
- Monsoon cooling → less bird stress → better FCR
- Post-heat demand recovery

**Negative factors:**
- Transport disruptions in flood-prone areas (Gorakhpur, Deoria, Maharajganj)
- Wet litter challenges → disease risk
- Consumer purchasing power temporarily reduced in flood-hit areas

**Price expectation:** ₹155–₹172/kg. Wide range because monsoon timing uncertain.

### September–October 2026: Pre-Festival Build

- Navratri (October) — *mixed demand* (some vegetarian fasting, but non-fasting population large)
- Onam, Ganesh Chaturthi — demand from South India supported markets last year
- Bihar demand: Post-Chhath Puja (October/November) surge

**Price expectation:** ₹165–₹180/kg. This is the build-up window.

**Strategy:** Farmers who place birds in August for October harvest likely to see good returns.

### November–December 2026: Peak Window

- Winter demand peak (cold weather → meat consumption ↑)
- Eid ul-Adha (likely November/December 2026 — confirm Islamic calendar)
- Christmas-adjacent demand
- Reduced supply (some farmers took production breaks in monsoon)

**Price expectation:** ₹175–₹200/kg. Possibly ₹200+ if no major disease event.

**This is the single best window of 2026 for broiler farmers.**

## 4 Key Factors to Watch in 2026

### Factor 1: HPAI Risk

The most important wild-card for UP poultry in 2026.

**Current situation:** HPAI H5N1 remains active in India. Adjacent states (Bihar, Uttarakhand) have seen periodic detections. The Gorakhpur belt's dense bird population makes it vulnerable.

**What to watch:**
- DAHDF bulletins — follow at dahd.nic.in
- FlockIQ disease risk score for your farm
- Any sudden arrivals reduction at nearby mandis (early signal)

**Farmer action if HPAI alert received:**
1. Do not panic-sell immediately (first alerts often proximate, not local)
2. Check your batch's Day number — if Day 30+, seriously consider selling
3. If Day 15–25: Enhance biosecurity, monitor mortality daily
4. Trust an early-warning system — don't wait for news to spread through WhatsApp groups

---

### Factor 2: Feed Costs — Maize and Soya

Feed = 65–70% of your production cost. Small price changes have large effects.

**2026 feed cost outlook:**

| Feed Ingredient | 2025 Avg Price | 2026 H1 Actual | H2 2026 Expectation |
|-----------------|---------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Maize | ₹21.5/kg | ₹20.8/kg | ₹19–23/kg (kharif harvest dependent) |
| Soya Meal | ₹46/kg | ₹44.5/kg | ₹42–50/kg (US crop, currency) |
| Layer Feed compound | ₹32/kg | ₹31.2/kg | ₹29–34/kg |

**Key event:** Kharif maize harvest (September–October) typically depresses maize prices. If 2026 kharif season is good (normal monsoon), feed costs will ease in Q4 → better margins.

**IMD 2026 monsoon forecast:** Normal to above-normal (as of May 2026) — positive for kharif.

---

### Factor 3: Andhra Pradesh/Telangana Supply

AP and Telangana together produce ~35% of India's total broiler output. When they have a production surplus, reefers (refrigerated trucks) supply UP markets at competitive prices, pressuring local farmer prices.

**2026 situation:**
- AP poultry sector: growing, with several large integrated players expanding
- Interstate supply to UP: UP markets see AP supply particularly in lean demand months (April–June, August–September)
- Impact: Creates a ₹5–₹8/kg floor competition for Gorakhpur-belt farmers in off-peak months

**Watch:** AGMARKNET's Hyderabad and Vijayawada mandi prices — if they're high, AP supply to UP is less. If they're low, more AP birds come to UP.

---

### Factor 4: Rural Consumer Purchasing Power

UP's rural consumption of broiler meat is elastic — it responds to agricultural income.

- **Good rabi harvest 2025-26:** Yes — wheat production was robust in UP (APEDA data)
- **MGNREGA wages:** Increased in 2025-26 — positive for rural consumption
- **Kharif 2026:** Early signs positive (good rainfall forecast)

**Implication:** Rural demand in UP in H2 2026 is expected to be good. This supports prices.

## किसानों के लिए 2026 Strategy

### Conservative Strategy (Risk-averse)

- Avoid new batch placements in June–July (HPAI season + monsoon)
- Maximize November–December window — place in September
- Use sell signals actively in Q3–Q4
- FCR target <1.85 for each batch (feed cost control)

### Aggressive Strategy (For experienced farmers)

- Place batch in July (early monsoon cooling → better FCR)
- Harvest target: September–October (pre-Diwali)
- Use disease early-warning system actively
- Follow-up batch for November–December peak

### Both strategies share:

1. **Daily log tracking** — know your FCR in real-time
2. **Disease alert subscription** — not negotiable given HPAI risk
3. **Sell signal discipline** — don't let traders make the decision for you
