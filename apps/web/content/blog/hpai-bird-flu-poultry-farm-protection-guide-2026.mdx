<h2>संक्षेप में:</h2>
<p>HPAI H5N1 (Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza) commercial poultry farms के लिए सबसे destructive disease है — market panic, price crash, और transport restrictions का मुख्य कारण। Prevention के 5 स्तंभ: (1) Wild bird exposure रोकें, (2) Entry points control करें, (3) Daily health monitoring, (4) Early warning system use करें, (5) Response protocol ready रखें। 2026 में Gorakhpur belt में proximity alerts आए हैं — precautions अभी critical हैं।</p>

<hr>

<h2>HPAI क्या है? किसानों के लिए Simple Explanation</h2>

<p><strong>HPAI = Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza</strong></p>

<p>सरल भाषा में: यह एक highly infectious virus है जो मुर्गियों और अन्य poultry को तेज़ी से मारता है। एक infected flock में 24–48 घंटे में 80–100% mortality possible है।</p>

<h2>HPAI से क्या होता है: किसान का नुकसान</h2>

<p>HPAI का financial impact दो तरफा होता है:</p>

<h3>Direct Loss (अगर आपका farm infected हो)</h3>

<ul>
<li>सभी पक्षी cull (मार दिए जाते हैं) — <strong>100% flock loss</strong></li>
<li>Government compensation: ₹100–₹120/bird (Uttaranchal rate as reference, UP rates vary) — मंडी price का 40–50%</li>
<li>Farm quarantine: 21 days — <strong>next batch नहीं रख सकते</strong></li>
<li>Equipment disinfection, downtime costs</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Example loss (10,000 birds):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Market value: 10,000 × 2.0 kg × ₹165 = ₹33,00,000</li>
<li>Government compensation: 10,000 × ₹110 = ₹11,00,000</li>
<li><strong>Net direct loss: ₹22,00,000+</strong></li>
</ul>

<h3>Indirect Loss (अगर adjacent area में outbreak हो)</h3>

<p><strong>यही वह situation है जो ज़्यादा UP farmers face करते हैं।</strong></p>

<p>Even if your farm is NOT infected:</p>
<ol>
<li>Market price crashes: ₹15–₹25/kg immediate drop (panic selling)</li>
<li>Transport restrictions: Buyers don't come to your area</li>
<li>Demand collapse: Consumers stop buying chicken temporarily</li>
<li>Your healthy birds are stuck — every day costs feed money</li>
</ol>

<p><strong>Example loss (20,000 birds, adjacent district outbreak):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Price drop: ₹162 → ₹145 (₹17/kg loss)</li>
<li>Total weight: 44,000 kg</li>
<li><strong>Indirect price loss: ₹7,48,000</strong></li>
</ul>

<h2>HPAI कैसे फैलता है: किसानों के लिए जानना ज़रूरी</h2>

<h3>Primary Route: Wild Birds (सबसे बड़ा risk)</h3>

<p>HPAI H5N1 का main reservoir wild waterfowl है — geese, ducks, migratory birds।</p>

<p>Gorakhpur belt के लिए specific concern:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ram Garh Tal (Gorakhpur):</strong> एक major wetland — migratory birds आते हैं October–February</li>
<li><strong>Rapti, Rohini, Kuwano rivers:</strong> Bird habitats जो farms के पास हैं</li>
<li><strong>Bihar wetlands adjacent:</strong> Kosi, Gandak river systems</li>
</ul>

<h3>Tertiary: Contaminated Water/Feed</h3>

<p>Stagnant water sources near farms can be contaminated by wild bird droppings. Open feed storage can attract wild birds.</p>

<h2>5 Levels of HPAI Prevention: Practical Guide</h2>

<h3>Level 1: Physical Barrier — Wild Bird Exclusion</h3>

<p><strong>Minimum standards:</strong></p>

<p>✅ <strong>Bird netting on all shed openings</strong> (mesh size ≤ 20mm)</p>
<ul>
<li>Side walls: Full height netting — no gaps</li>
<li>Roof vents: Protected with fine mesh</li>
<li>Entry doors: Double-door system or curtain barrier</li>
</ul>

<p>✅ <strong>No open water bodies within 500m</strong> — drain or fence off</p>
<ul>
<li>Check for seasonal water accumulation near farm</li>
<li>Rice fields nearby: Extra vigilance September–November</li>
</ul>

<p>✅ <strong>Feed storage: Covered, bird-proof containers</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Open gunny bags attract sparrows, crows</li>
<li>Rodents also carry pathogens</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Cost estimate (shed 15,000 birds, ~5,000 sq ft):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Bird netting: ₹25,000–₹40,000</li>
<li>Double-door entry system: ₹8,000–₹15,000</li>
<li><strong>One-time investment. Protection: every batch.</strong></li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h3>Level 2: Biosecurity Protocol — Entry Control</h3>

<p><strong>Foot-and-mouth: the biggest contamination risk is footwear.</strong></p>

<p><strong>Must-do steps for every person entering the shed:</strong></p>

<ol>
<li><strong>Foot dip tray at entry:</strong> Disinfectant solution (Virkon S, 1% formalin, or bleach solution)
<ul>
<li>Minimum 3–5 cm depth</li>
<li>Change solution every 24 hours or when visibly dirty</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Dedicated farm footwear:</strong> एक pair of boots that NEVER leaves the farm
<ul>
<li>For workers: old sandals/chappals kept at farm entry</li>
<li>For visitors: disposable boot covers (₹3/pair)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Hand washing station:</strong> Soap and water before entering shed area
<ul>
<li>If possible: hand sanitizer (70% alcohol) as well</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Visitor log:</strong> Date, name, contact, previous farm visited (last 48 hours)
<ul>
<li>Red flag: visitor came from a farm in HPAI alert zone</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Vehicle:</strong> Don't allow feed/delivery vehicles inside shed area
<ul>
<li>Unload at farm perimeter, carry into shed separately</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>

<p><strong>Daily record:</strong> Note any unusual visitors, vehicles, or equipment that entered.</p>

<hr>

<h3>Level 3: Daily Health Monitoring</h3>

<p><strong>The earlier you spot HPAI, the more options you have.</strong></p>

<p><strong>Daily morning check (15 minutes):</strong></p>

<p>Walk through the shed. Note:</p>

<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Observation</th>
<th>Normal</th>
<th>Concern</th>
<th>Emergency</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Dead birds</td>
<td>&lt; 0.5%/day cumulative</td>
<td>0.5–1%</td>
<td>&gt; 1%/day</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Behaviour</td>
<td>Active, eating</td>
<td>Lethargic, huddling</td>
<td>Sudden mass death</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Respiratory</td>
<td>Silent, normal</td>
<td>Occasional sneezing</td>
<td>Gasping, coughing across flock</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Head/wattle</td>
<td>Normal colour</td>
<td>Slight bluish tint</td>
<td>Dark purple/black, swollen</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Eyes</td>
<td>Clear</td>
<td>Mild watery</td>
<td>Swollen, opaque</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Eggs (layers)</td>
<td>Normal production</td>
<td>Drop &gt;10%</td>
<td>Drop &gt;30% sudden</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<p><strong>HPAI specific signs (very fast onset):</strong></p>

<ul>
<li>Sudden death of many birds in 24–48 hours</li>
<li>Dark purple/cyanotic comb and wattles</li>
<li>Nervous signs (trembling, loss of coordination)</li>
<li>Complete feed refusal</li>
<li>Respiratory distress across flock</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>If 3+ of these signs appear: Call vet IMMEDIATELY. Do NOT wait.</strong></p>

<h3>Level 5: Response Protocol — अगर HPAI suspect हो</h3>

<p><strong>यह सबसे ज़रूरी plan है जो सबके पास होना चाहिए — लेकिन किसी के पास नहीं होता।</strong></p>

<p><strong>अगर आपको अपने shed में HPAI signs दिखें:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Step 1: Isolation (Immediate)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Shed band करें — कोई अंदर नहीं, कोई बाहर नहीं</li>
<li>अपने workers को shed से बाहर निकालें</li>
<li>कोई भी पक्षी shed से बाहर नहीं जाना चाहिए</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Step 2: Phone Call (Within 30 minutes)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>अपने district का Chief Veterinary Officer (CVO) call करें</li>
<li>UP Animal Husbandry Department helpline: 1800-180-5141 (toll-free)</li>
<li>अपने integrator को inform करें</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Step 3: Document (Before calling vet)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Dead bird count note करें</li>
<li>Video/photos लें (symptoms, dead birds)</li>
<li>पिछले 48 घंटे का movement log निकालें (कौन आया, कौन गया)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Step 4: Do NOT:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Dead birds को खुले में न फेंकें</li>
<li>Neighbors को पक्षी न दें</li>
<li>किसी भी transport को farm से बाहर न जाने दें</li>
<li>Social media पर post न करें (panic spreading)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Step 5: Wait for Vet</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Vet आएगा, samples लेगा</li>
<li>24–48 hours में results</li>
<li>अगर positive: government protocol follows</li>
</ul>

<h2>Biosecurity Checklist: Monthly Audit</h2>

<p>Print करें और farm पर लगाएं।</p>

<p><strong>Weekly:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>[ ] Foot dip solution changed (Monday, Thursday)</li>
<li>[ ] Bird netting integrity check — no tears, gaps</li>
<li>[ ] Dead bird disposal done properly (deep burial, lime)</li>
<li>[ ] Visitor log updated</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Monthly:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>[ ] Feed storage area cleaned and bird-proofed</li>
<li>[ ] Drainage around shed clear (no stagnant water)</li>
<li>[ ] Worker training refreshed (entry protocol reminder)</li>
<li>[ ] Emergency vet contact numbers updated</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Before each batch placement:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>[ ] Full shed disinfection completed</li>
<li>[ ] 14-day rest period observed</li>
<li>[ ] Bird netting inspected and repaired</li>
<li>[ ] DAHDF/FlockIQ disease risk check for current district status</li>
</ul>
