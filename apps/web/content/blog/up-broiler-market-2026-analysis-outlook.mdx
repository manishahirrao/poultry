<p>UP का ब्रॉयलर market 2026 में mixed outlook दे रहा है। Q1 (Jan–Mar) मजबूत रहा — ₹170–185/kg range। Q2 (Apr–Jun) में गर्मी और HPAI proximity से pressure। Q3 (Jul–Sep) monsoon के बाद demand recovery expected। Feed costs stable (soybean और maize prices controlled)। HPAI risk medium — Gorakhpur belt में proximity alerts आए लेकिन major outbreak नहीं। सबसे बड़ी opportunity: Kharif crop season के बाद October–December window।</p>

<h2>UP Poultry Industry: Size और Importance</h2>

<p>उत्तर प्रदेश भारत के poultry production में एक significant player है।</p>

<p><strong>Key facts (DADF 2024-25):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>UP में commercial broiler production: ~1.4 billion kg annually (estimate)</li>
<li>UP का national share: approximately 7–8% of India's total broiler output</li>
<li>Major producing districts: Gorakhpur, Bareilly, Lucknow-Unnao belt, Agra-Mathura belt</li>
<li><strong>Gorakhpur belt</strong> (Gorakhpur, Deoria, Kushinagar, Basti, Sant Kabir Nagar, Maharajganj): UP की poultry production का ~12–15%</li>
</ul>

<p>UP का broiler market कई कारणों से unique है:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Large local demand:</strong> 25 million+ population in Gorakhpur division alone</li>
<li><strong>Proximity to Bihar:</strong> Bihar को significant supply जाती है</li>
<li><strong>Andhra Pradesh influence:</strong> AP और Telangana से UP में significant interstate supply</li>
<li><strong>Festival concentration:</strong> Eid, Holi, Dussehra — major demand peaks</li>
<li><strong>Seasonal weather extremes:</strong> May–July में 45°C+, December–January में 5°C — both extremes affect production</li>
</ol>

<h2>2025: पिछले साल क्या हुआ? (Recap)</h2>

<p>2025 का साल UP poultry किसानों के लिए volatile था।</p>

<h3>Q1 2025 (January–March): Strong Start</h3>

<ul>
<li>Average Gorakhpur APMC price: ₹168–185/kg</li>
<li>Demand driver: Winter peak demand + Holi festival (March)</li>
<li>Supply: Normal — no major disruptions</li>
<li><strong>Result:</strong> Good margins for farmers who sold correctly</li>
</ul>

<h3>Q2 2025 (April–June): Heat Stress + HPAI Scare</h3>

<ul>
<li>April: Sudden heat wave — production disrupted in Deoria, Kushinagar</li>
<li>May: HPAI H5N1 detected in Maharajganj district → 48-hour price crash (₹182 → ₹151 in 3 days)</li>
<li>June: Market recovery partial — ₹158–165 range</li>
<li><strong>Result:</strong> Farmers who had early alert (FlockIQ) avoided crash. Others lost ₹15–₹25/kg</li>
</ul>

<h3>Q3 2025 (July–September): Monsoon Recovery</h3>

<ul>
<li>July: Post-HPAI clearance, market gradually recovering</li>
<li>August–September: Demand steady — Onam, Ganesh Chaturthi consumption</li>
<li>Price range: ₹155–₹172/kg</li>
<li><strong>Result:</strong> Moderate. Transport challenges in flood-affected areas (Gorakhpur floods August)</li>
</ul>

<h3>Q4 2025 (October–December): Best Quarter</h3>

<ul>
<li>October: Dussehra/Navratri period — cautious demand (partial vegetarian festival)</li>
<li>November–December: Winter peak demand. Eid ul-Adha period.</li>
<li>Price range: ₹175–₹196/kg (December peak)</li>
<li><strong>Result:</strong> Best quarter. Farmers with birds ready in November–December did very well.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>2025 full-year average Gorakhpur price: ₹168/kg (estimated)</strong></p>

<li>Demand: Steady — no major festival boost until Eid</li>

<h2>H2 2026 Outlook: क्या उम्मीद करें?</h2>

<h3>July–August 2026: Monsoon Impact</h3>

<p><strong>Positive factors:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Monsoon cooling → less bird stress → better FCR</li>
<li>Post-heat demand recovery</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Negative factors:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Transport disruptions in flood-prone areas (Gorakhpur, Deoria, Maharajganj)</li>
<li>Wet litter challenges → disease risk</li>
<li>Consumer purchasing power temporarily reduced in flood-hit areas</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Price expectation:</strong> ₹155–₹172/kg. Wide range because monsoon timing uncertain.</p>

<h3>September–October 2026: Pre-Festival Build</h3>

<ul>
<li>Navratri (October) — <em>mixed demand</em> (some vegetarian fasting, but non-fasting population large)</li>
<li>Onam, Ganesh Chaturthi — demand from South India supported markets last year</li>
<li>Bihar demand: Post-Chhath Puja (October/November) surge</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Price expectation:</strong> ₹165–₹180/kg. This is the build-up window.</p>

<p><strong>Strategy:</strong> Farmers who place birds in August for October harvest likely to see good returns.</p>

<h3>November–December 2026: Peak Window</h3>

<ul>
<li>Winter demand peak (cold weather → meat consumption ↑)</li>
<li>Eid ul-Adha (likely November/December 2026 — confirm Islamic calendar)</li>
<li>Christmas-adjacent demand</li>
<li>Reduced supply (some farmers took production breaks in monsoon)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Price expectation:</strong> ₹175–₹200/kg. Possibly ₹200+ if no major disease event.</p>

<p><strong>This is the single best window of 2026 for broiler farmers.</strong></p>

<h2>4 Key Factors to Watch in 2026</h2>

<h3>Factor 1: HPAI Risk</h3>

<p>The most important wild-card for UP poultry in 2026.</p>

<p><strong>Current situation:</strong> HPAI H5N1 remains active in India. Adjacent states (Bihar, Uttarakhand) have seen periodic detections. The Gorakhpur belt's dense bird population makes it vulnerable.</p>

<p><strong>What to watch:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>DAHDF bulletins — follow at dahd.nic.in</li>
<li>FlockIQ disease risk score for your farm</li>
<li>Any sudden arrivals reduction at nearby mandis (early signal)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Farmer action if HPAI alert received:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Do not panic-sell immediately (first alerts often proximate, not local)</li>
<li>Check your batch's Day number — if Day 30+, seriously consider selling</li>
<li>If Day 15–25: Enhance biosecurity, monitor mortality daily</li>
<li>Trust an early-warning system — don't wait for news to spread through WhatsApp groups</li>
</ol>

<h3>Factor 3: Andhra Pradesh/Telangana Supply</h3>

<p>AP and Telangana together produce ~35% of India's total broiler output. When they have a production surplus, reefers (refrigerated trucks) supply UP markets at competitive prices, pressuring local farmer prices.</p>

<p><strong>2026 situation:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>AP poultry sector: growing, with several large integrated players expanding</li>
<li>Interstate supply to UP: UP markets see AP supply particularly in lean demand months (April–June, August–September)</li>
<li>Impact: Creates a ₹5–₹8/kg floor competition for Gorakhpur-belt farmers in off-peak months</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Watch:</strong> AGMARKNET's Hyderabad and Vijayawada mandi prices — if they're high, AP supply to UP is less. If they're low, more AP birds come to UP.</p>

<hr>

<h3>Factor 4: Rural Consumer Purchasing Power</h3>

<p>UP's rural consumption of broiler meat is elastic — it responds to agricultural income.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Good rabi harvest 2025-26:</strong> Yes — wheat production was robust in UP (APEDA data)</li>
<li><strong>MGNREGA wages:</strong> Increased in 2025-26 — positive for rural consumption</li>
<li><strong>Kharif 2026:</strong> Early signs positive (good rainfall forecast)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Implication:</strong> Rural demand in UP in H2 2026 is expected to be good. This supports prices.</p>

<h2>किसानों के लिए 2026 Strategy</h2>

<h3>Conservative Strategy (Risk-averse)</h3>

<ul>
<li>Avoid new batch placements in June–July (HPAI season + monsoon)</li>
<li>Maximize November–December window — place in September</li>
<li>Use sell signals actively in Q3–Q4</li>
<li>FCR target &lt;1.85 for each batch (feed cost control)</li>
</ul>

<h3>Aggressive Strategy (For experienced farmers)</h3>

<ul>
<li>Place batch in July (early monsoon cooling → better FCR)</li>
<li>Harvest target: September–October (pre-Diwali)</li>
<li>Use disease early-warning system actively</li>
<li>Follow-up batch for November–December peak</li>
</ul>

<h3>Both strategies share:</h3>

<ol>
<li><strong>Daily log tracking</strong> — know your FCR in real-time</li>
<li><strong>Disease alert subscription</strong> — not negotiable given HPAI risk</li>
<li><strong>Sell signal discipline</strong> — don't let traders make the decision for you</li>
</ol>
